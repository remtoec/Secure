# Editorial source and content design

## Product role

The app is a companion and trailer for Bookfort's sharing of Amir Levine's
*Secure*. It has three audiences:

1. Participants use the questionnaire and map before the sharing.
2. Other Bookfort members get a concise glimpse of the book's central frame,
   then an invitation to contact the organiser and join Bookfort Zone for the
   sharing record and notes.
3. Interested readers are pointed to the book for the complete argument,
   examples, and practices.

The app must remain useful without becoming a compressed substitute for the
sharing or the book.

## Source hierarchy

1. Amir Levine, *Secure: The Revolutionary Guide to Creating a Secure Life*
   (Tarcher, 2026) is the authority for the author's claims, terminology,
   examples, chapter structure, questionnaire presentation, and Collaborative
   Assessment Scale.
2. The whole-book chapter summary is an orientation aid. It helps locate an
   idea but is not an authority for quotation or fine distinctions.
3. Chapter 5's agent-friendly extract is a working aid for the questionnaire
   and topography exercise; it must be checked against the book.
4. The ECR-RS research scale is the measurement source. Its English items stay
   verbatim and its Chinese translation preserves what each item measures.
5. Bookfort's prose supplies navigation, privacy explanations, invitations,
   and discussion prompts. These must not be attributed to Levine.

## The glimpse the landing page should give

Levine treats attachment as a context-sensitive and environmentally responsive
system. Childhood is one influence, but the brain continues to learn from the
relationships and small interactions it encounters now. Exclusion, emotional
blankness, and unpredictability keep social-surveillance circuitry active;
consistent and responsive experiences can gradually support greater security.

The topography makes that argument visible: one person can occupy different
positions with a parent, partner, friend, or colleague. The result is a map of
responses in relationships, not a fixed personality verdict or a diagnosis.

## Information boundary

### Keep in the app

- The Chapter 5 situation hook, in shortened form.
- General and person-specific ECR-RS questionnaires.
- Anxiety and avoidance scores, quadrant, map, retesting, records, and privacy.
- A short, source-faithful interpretation of each quadrant.
- One Bookfort discussion prompt that hands the result into the sharing.
- The Chapter 11 Collaborative Assessment Scale with faithful items and score
  ranges.
- Links to Levine's official *Secure* page and Bookfort's contact route.

### Leave mainly for the sharing or book

- Full explanations of Cyberball, stillfacing, CARRP, and SIMIs.
- Style-specific programs from Chapters 6–8.
- The protest-regret cycle, Appendix Rule, Wall Tennis with Love, secure-village
  practice, and real-time coaching rules.
- Chapter summaries, case histories, neuroscience arguments, and therapeutic
  applications.

Only the short definitions needed to answer a questionnaire should appear
inside the app.

## Voice and translation

Chinese copy follows `docs/translation-principles.md`: natural Hong Kong
Traditional Chinese, concrete actions, clear causal movement, and no inflated
therapeutic or philosophical tone. Each attachment position retains its
adaptive logic and its cost. The prose should sound warm, scientifically
curious, and hopeful without promising a diagnosis or cure.

## Result handoff

The result page gives the score, a concise interpretation, and one question:
does this response appear across relationships, or does it emerge around
particular people and particular everyday interactions? It does not prescribe
the later chapters' solutions. That conversation belongs to the sharing.
